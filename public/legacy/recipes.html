Friday, January 16th, 2009

 

Jonah and the Big Blue Balloon

            The big blue balloon looks like a mini watermelon.  A blue watermelon.  It’s got a handle on it that was once used to hold on to while Jonah strived valiantly to tame the meeting of his fist with the balloon.  “Once,” because it has since broke.  Twice.  The first time the elastic handle snapped, it was able to be tied back into a handle that was now smaller –with less give in the balloon’s bouncing-out, before being pulled back to an anxious fist.  This still afforded a sizeable amount of fun for Jonah – whose world revolves around chocolate milk and physics.  Less freedom in the balloon’s movement didn’t necessarily mean less fun. 

            The balloon’s elastic handle broke once more.  This isn’t Jonah’s fault.  He doesn’t know that he ultimately has control over whether the elastic band breaks or not – to an extent.  A 95 percent extent.  The elastic handle actually didn’t break.  This time, it simply became untied from the first handiwork.  This is important.  It isn’t Jonah’s responsibilty to fix the balloon’s handle when it breaks.  He hasn’t yet learned how. 

            The balloon is a big deal.  When Jonah is with the big blue balloon – nothing else exists.  The blue balloon is his world.  When the balloon first received its first breaths of air, thus bringing it to life, Jonah watched in rapture.  Jonah watched as this creature of the laws of physics bounced and floated around in certain ways in response to the way outside forces acted upon it.  Jonah learned much in the first ten minutes of his observations.  He may have learned most – but definitely not all. 

            Sometimes, others join in Jonah’s excitement and master the balloon’s course and velocity in ways Jonah wasn’t aware of being possible - until he watches them.  Jonah recognizes that this level of control comes from traveling many times the path from the heavens to the earth – a path that is at the core of the balloons descent each time it loses energy from the force of the punch and gently glides back down to the floor.  This never happens without Jonah watching every moment and every subtle variation in the balloon’s magnificent fall from grace.  His attention is undivided – as long as it is not divided.  Anything else but the balloon is a distraction.  Nothing becomes more important than Jonah and the fate of the big blue balloon.  Nothing, yet. 

            Jonah is getting better at controlling the way the balloon reacts to his deliberately delicate hands.  The big blue balloon doesn’t seem as big as it was at first.  It also doesn’t seem as unpredictable.  Jonah is now developing more elaborate ways to bring his balloon in communion with the other elements of his immediate environment.  There is no limit to the possibilities he sees, in this, the most beautiful relationship, he feels. 

            Sometimes Jonah loses control and the balloon rapidly beats against his hand in a manner utterly chaotic.  These times are becoming less.  Jonah has patience.  He wants to become the balloon’s master.  He IS the balloon’s master – and feels responsible for the balloon’s actions.  Some may blame the balloon for their actions.  Not Jonah.  Yet. 

            The big blue balloon is Jonah’s teacher.  Yet Jonah is the big blue balloon’s master.  The big blue balloon will always be waiting for Jonah to affect it with his actions.  The big blue balloon yearns for Jonah’s attention - because it is only through this interaction that the big blue balloon is given meaning.  It is only through this interaction that the big blue balloon is brought into existence.  Jonah is a picture of the action working through him.  The big blue balloon is also a picture of Jonah’s action.  Jonah and the big blue balloon share a special connection.  They become each other.  And so Jonah sees himself in the balloon’s reflection.  Jonah sees his eyes reflected in the shiny balloon.  He looks closely and sees the balloon’s reflection in the reflection of his eyes.  His eyes reflect the wonder of his own being and Jonah realizes the greatest truth.  A truth embedded in the infinite nature of Man seeing the reflection of the big blue balloon in the reflection of his very own eyes.

 

Saturday, January 3rd, 2009

 

Death can be a pretty scary thought for us.  When I speak of “death” here, I speak of the physical body’s death.  Death is THE most fearful thought one can grapple with – but ONLY when the mind and body are disunited. 

            When the mind and body are not in harmony, everything that the body does can be scary – because the mind is not in control.  Are not our bodies tools for us to use?  What happens when tools develop a “mind of their own.”  It’s just like the struggle between mand and technology.  What happens when robots begin to act independently of the human spirit?  It’s a frightening thought – but only BECAUSE the human spirit is missing.  This is almost exactly the situation of one’s body when it is acting independently of the spirit which is the exact thing which animates the body.  Without the spirit, the body is lifeless – literally.  It may be as if a pre-programmed robot, simply executing ingrained actions.

            It’s actually shockingly parallel – it’s scary.  A physical body with no human spirit consciously penetrating it has a tendency – even a nature – to think and act based on numbers – based on calculations – just like a robot!  One’s body, when lacking the clarity of awareness the spirit provides, operates according to assumptions – thinks in terms of theory, in terms of guidelines, in terms of definitions with which to confine itself in – just like a robot!  It seems to me that this archetypal struggle between man and technology is actually rooted in something much deeper – something much more personal.  The struggle between Man’s Body and his Mind.  To be able to conduct the body like an orchestra of microscopic machines that communicate in perfect harmony with each other – this is Life.  To truly wear your body like a suit and have it interact with the universe in a way that is a perfect extension of your thoughts, feelings, and will – an extension that, is indeed a perfect representation of the human spirit behind it – and not a rebellious robot that usurps its creator’s control.  This is Life. 

            And so I feel like I can now say that nothing ever should be done while the mind and body are disunited.  The most important thing – above all else – in all circumstances – is unity.  Whenever there is disunity, whether between people, or between a person’s mind and body, or between an action and the person doing the action – whenever there is disunity – it breaks the tendency of the human experience to strivetowards order and beauty.  When there is unity on all fronts – unity between people involved in an experience, and unity between the minds and bodies of the people involved in an experience – the universe unites with itself in that moment and God meets himself.

 

Friday, January 2, 2009

 

I found this poem waiting for me in my email’s inbox today.

A Prayer for Christmas
 
All through the darkness of the holy days approaching
May we prove our goodwill by accepting without complaint
       the conditions in which we find ourselves.
May we manifest our goodwill in mildest ways of love by trying to
       improve those conditions.
 
Within ourselves may we establish peace, a deepening quietness,
             a holy stillness of anticipation wherein we offer God
             unceasingly our thanks for His absolute gift,
His greatest of possible gifts, His gift of being:  Creative being
             in life, creative being in death, being in a universe of
            being, with all the fellowship of beings higher and lower.
 
May each offer his single and unified heart to receive whatever
           addition of divine light, divine life, divine love God sees
           fit to grant him in his imperfection.
May each heart use what awakens in it in this time of consecration
          to make a new beginning:  A turning in all things towards
          the eternal.
 
                                                                                       -John F. Gardner

Beautiful.  And so the new year is here.  I vow to dedicate this year to cultivating beauty in everything, everywhere, and with everybody. 

            The “New Year” is a very interesting occurrence.  Fundamentally – there really is nothing “new” about it – insofar as every single day is a “new” day.  So what’s the big deal?  I feel it has to do with humankind’s perception of time and how it relates to the Earth’s overall mission.  The completion of another year reminds us all that, yes, we are mortal, and yes, our physical bodies have a limited time on this Earth, and yes, there are certain things we must do while here that we must not forget about.  In a sense it’s like a wakeup call – and I see it absolutely having that effect.  People make “new year’s resolutions” – indicating a rekindling of the motivation to be passionate about life.  A motivation we should always have but too oftentimes let wither while we watch our TV, sleep in our beds, and drink our morning coffee and our evening wine.  These things are important – yet secondary.  I guess I might also mention that the passion for life withers also while we eat our snacks and chase our romances.  It withers while we read our spirituality books and eat our organic food.  It withers while we work out at the gym and check our savings account balance online.  If every day was the beginning of a new epoch.  An epoch of greatness.  Would this stimulate the passion we seem to have trouble sustaining ? 

            We mustn’t become comfortable.  We mustn’t become convenient.  We must become ourselves.  Awake.  And so awake it makes us uncomfortable insomniacs – until we become used to it – and it becomes a gift – and we learn how to use this gift to watch over people while they sleep.  And wake them up before anything happens to them – even if they fall right back asleep and never thank you.  Our eyes may become tired – but the heart never tires.  A beating heart does not slow as you come closer to needing sleep.  Words don’t lose their meaning until the meaning loses the one speaking the words.  Don’t get lost.  Pay close attention.  Be rigorous with your passion.  Keep your eyes on the meaning and it will choose you as its messenger.  Be a light so bright it can wake a person up out of a coma.  A light so bright you must place yourself at the center – for to stand in the periphery stings your eyes that you may not see. 

Be precise.

Don’t be frivolous with your words.

But don’t be mundane with them either.

Be extravagant with them.

Be delightfully witty and eloquently quaint.

Be simple and precise yet thorough and encompassing.

Be this entire passage – punctuated in three words.

Be Unconditional Love.

Tuesday, December 23, 2008

 

     And so I find myself typing these first words out of an impulse to free myself from inner demons - and perhaps, maybe, hopefully, to free others.   I won't edit what I write, and am choosing instead to let words, as expressions of thought, feeling, and will, flow - unencumbered.  I am slowly coming to grips with my own True Self.  Coming slowly to it in this moment.  Life is a constant coming to your True Self.  Anything less than the most gloriously clear and radiant awareness is not your True Self.  One's True Self is only ever this.  This gloriously clear and radiant awareness that allows you to bind everything externally to what's going on internally. 

     One may be simple and say that the True Self is the self that is always existing in the present moment - and is never distracted by even the tiniest whisper of a thought.  The entire experience of the present moment becomes the only thought.  But it is not a thought you dictate with your own will.  One may sit and "stare off" and think about all sorts of things.  One may control, more or less, the flow of thoughts - the subject of the thoughts.  Try it.  One will quickly find that this incessant thinking is happening, "in the background," almost at all times.  For some it's much more debilitating than for others.  In which I mean that some are deeper into the "conversation with themself" than others.  I say "debilitating" because this constant thinking does just that - it weakens the True Self's ability to shine through.  The True Self is a thought that is the very moment.  It is a thought that isn't confined within your mind but is actually what is happening in front of you as you are experiencing it.  It is unchanging, yet is constantly becoming the reflection of the present moment's image. 

     It's much too easy to read these words and to think about them too deeply - to get caught up in the words describing the phenomenon and to lose sight of the phenomenon itself.  The True Self is the most simple thing there is.  It is absolute simplicity.  It is absolutely the most simple thing you can possibly imagine, yet it requires no imagination.  It is a state of mind with no outside forces of thought.  It is purely YOU - but not the "You" you may be accustomed to.  The True Self is not a "You" that includes likes and dislikes, interests, quirks of personality, skills or weaknesses.  But this is the most important point - no weaknesses.  The True Self is simply a child of the present moment.  It is perfectly spontaneous - yet perfectly unwavering.  The True Self never thinks about itself - because it doesn't recognize itself as being apart from all else that is happening.  It doesn't recognize itself as being separate from others' experiences.  Others' experiences are a part of the present moment's snapshot and so are a part of the experience that is your True Self. 

   And here I find myself getting lost within my own symbols - symbols used to represent a reality.  Don't ever let the symbols become the point.  They never were the point - yet something can come in and start to make you belive that these words are the point.  That it's important to capture reality so it's not lost.  There is forever the yearning for things to hold on to.  Memories, words, objects, people.  Words are no different.  There exists an enjoyment in collecting words and holding onto them.  Reading them for the sake of merely holding onto them within one's own being - as if to collect knowledge or "ways of saying."  But this is not the True Self's desire.  The True Self is utterly improvisational.  It perpetually finds ways to express itself that are specific to the present moment.  It is weary about writing down anything, knowing that what it said was meant for the moment it said it.  It becomes sad when others interpret its meaning - for the True Self is not something which is open to interpretation.  It is absolute in its nature.  It says what it means and it means what it says - always. 

     I realize that this depression I've found myself "thrown into" is not my True Self.  It has no cause outside of the thoughts which I give attention to.  These thoughts are of ill influence - and they have become much more than simply thoughts I am objectively aware of.   They have actually become me and therefore become brought to life in the physical world.  I have become the object to these thoughts - as opposed to viewing them objectively and recognizing their foreign nature.  These thoughts are not me.  They are merely clouding my judgement and tricking me into believing that they are indeed from me.  And so I act as if they are me.  And so I become a physical manifestation of the feeling of depression.  This is not me - and in the moment I realize that this heaviness is foreign - is the moment I realize I have the awareness and versatility to choose to let it in, or to keep it out.  This also becomes the moment in which I become free.
